Eric’s Personal Boundaries

Renata,

I am supportive of you, empathetic to you, and I do my share for you and for our family, Challenging these beliefs or disregarding my personal boundaries will cause me to disengage.

Personal boundaries:
  • Excessive criticism of me or another family member
  • Criticism of me or another family member in front of Amelia
  • Unilaterally reversing decisions that you and I have agreed on because Amelia is upset about the decision

I love you very much. I disengage at these times because our history demonstrates that attempting to remain engaged during these situations results in a larger conflict and makes it harder to reconnect later.

Eric


Frequently Asked Questions:

Question: Do you really do enough for me?

Answer: Yes, I do. You live a luxurious and low stress lifestyle in a beautiful home in a safe and wonderful neighborhood. You spend your leisure time creating an exceptional garden. These things are all possible because of the things I do for you. I also spend time each day doing things specifically for you and for our family.

Question: Do I really criticize you that much?

Answer: Yes, you do. If you truly uncertain how much you criticize me, reread the email and texts that you previously sent me when you are angry. I put sincere effort into my responses to those texts and email in hopes that one day you will go back and read them so that we will be able to improve our relationship. When you are upset, your text and email criticisms are painful. However, your in-person criticisms more painful and are truly harmful to us and our family.

Question: Are thing getting better?

Answer: Not really. We still go through these episodes at least every 1 to 3 weeks. During the episode you become angry and aggressive. Looking back at the “triggers” of these episodes (i.e. cheese stick wrapper on the counter, vacation plans, request to take a walk in the morning, etc.) your response was unjustifiable and out of proportion. On the other hand, setting boundaries and disengaging instead of arguing the topics has made a difference.

Question: Do I really view you differently from one day to the next?

Answer: From my perspective, your view of me can change dramatically from on hour to the next (with no warning or cause). I can be held in very high esteem during one interaction, and a short time later I am viewed as if I were a completely different person.

Sometimes it feels like there are identical twin Renatas. One who thinks I am the greatest person that ever lived. And her identical twin sister who is sure that I am the scum of the earth. And they switch places unexpectedly for a couple days every one to three weeks. The periodic visits of Renata’s twin are extremely painful for me, harmful to our marriage, and harmful for our family.

Question: All couples fight.

Answer: That’s not really a question, but my answer is that our level of fighting and your level of aggressive behavior is not healthy or normal in a marriage. Instead it is harmful to us, to our marriage, and to our family.

Question: What can we do to improve our relationship?

Answer: Mostly I think it will not change. Sadly, maintaining healthy boundaries and disengaging during conflicts are the only coping mechanisms that I have found during our/my search of improvements. I pray for more.
